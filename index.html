Two Missourians in Paris

19 years ago this June, my mother and I took our first trip together to Paris. Not Paris, MO, which is probably a fine place where we've never been. No, we actually went on an airplane from Lambert when it was still as active as an international airport, and flew non-stop to the "City of Lights". My mother had never been outside of the United States at the time, and we had never traveled alone together, so of course, an adventure ensued.

Here is an example.

My mother and I arrived at Paris with relatively little trouble. We flagged a taxi which turned out to be a late-model navy Mercedes-Benz sedan. We both laughed about our "luxurious" ride to city center; we certainly weren't in Kansas (Missouri) anymore! We arrive at our little hotel in the 6th district ("arrondissement"), We were grateful that we could squeeze ourselves and luggage into a miniscule elevator, but we were pleased with this amazing view over the rooftops of the area surrounding the Sorbonne, one of the world's oldest universities.

Like a million folks before us, Mom and I loved Paris because it was beautiful. We trekked all over the city together, speaking my high school french, and smiling and pointing. We ate voraciously, with the best food being cafe french fries, and streetcart savory crepes.

We toured museums, of course!

And churches

And parks!

Fortunately, we only had one public altercation, when my mother doubted my map reading skills. I also had my wallet stolen(completely my fault) at a Paris metro station. All in all, we midwesterners survived our Parisian odyssey, yearning for french fries forevermore.
